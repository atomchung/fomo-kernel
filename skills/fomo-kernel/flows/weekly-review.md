# Weekly review flow

Use when the Review Plan has `route=weekly_review`.

1. Read `state_snapshot` from the Review Plan. Do not scan the entire `~/.trade-coach` directory. If `state_snapshot.cadence.tier == "light"`, stop here and follow `flows/light-capture.md` instead of the remaining steps in this flow.
2. Begin the interpretation by reconciling against `prior_commitment`; when it is null, state that the previous review ended with no committed rule. Record that `prior_commitment` or `prior_skip` was actually presented following `references/interaction-delivery.md`. The renderer opens the card with the committed rule and that metric's then/now values verbatim; interpret the movement in the mirror, and do not compute a delta yourself.
3. Ask only items in `question_queue`, once, following the native-control, fixed-text, and presentation-trace protocols in `references/interaction-delivery.md`. Prepare already deduplicated them against active theses and add counts; do not ask raw engine `thesis_questions` again. For `add_thesis`, `headline_motive`, and `exit_consistency`, bind a grounded private surface through `review.py resume --question-surfaces`, then present the returned exact presentation; on any surface failure, keep the unchanged engine fallback. `due_revisit`, `rule_breach`, and recent-exit capture stay engine-rendered. An `exit_consistency` question is the answerable form of the Block-3 `[?]` exit-opportunity-cost observation: when the engine queues it, it names the same instruments/counts and asks whether the early exits were a deliberate rule, an emotional reaction, or an external constraint; the read-only panel then yields to it (`docs/output-contract.md`). When the queue carries `prior_exit_reason` or a matured revisit, record the matching memory presentation after showing that engine-owned context. Never change the route, queue, kind, priority, required status, canonical choices, payload gates, facts, or identities; invent facts; or use more than the one frozen clarification. A quoted thesis keeps its engine-declared voice, and one fact-grounded observation per review may still enter `observations` without changing the queue.
4. The queue contains one to three engine-ranked questions. A recent-exit question captures the user's reason without judging the outcome; a `skip` answer is saved and must not be asked again.
5. A `due_revisit` question replays the user's own recorded exit reason from the question payload verbatim; do not soften or reinterpret it. Present the swap comparison only from `compare` (missing prices stay listed in `needs_prices`; never estimate a return). Its `skip` is not saved â€” the same checkpoint legitimately returns next review. The private renderer joins a saved verdict back to this frozen comparison; the public renderer never consumes it.
6. `state_snapshot.market_context` and `horizon_markers` are frozen engine facts for the private card. Do not fetch prices again, calculate a new comparison, or turn a horizon marker into an unasked motive verdict.
7. When `state_snapshot.exit_backlog` is present, mention the aggregate pattern in one sentence at most; it is history for context, not a weekly interrogation list.
8. When `state_snapshot.problem_stats` is present, weave the top recurring problems into the mirror. `held_streak >= 2` stays silent; `skipped` periods are never claimed as held. A rule is called broken only after a `rule_breach` question qualifies the exact-period evidence.
9. A `rule_breach` question always offers `keep_tracking` and `exception`; it also offers `revise_rule` only when an engine metric can track a replacement for that same problem key. `exception` and `revise_rule` require a short note. If the user chooses `revise_rule`, use the one final commitment as the replacement and set `commitment.revises_rule_id` to the question's `rule_id`; never append a second independent revision. Prepare asks only the first unqualified breach or a later worsening.
10. Treat every `missing_thesis_positions` item as a new cycle or a historical thesis gap and fill it using the inference-first contract from the first-review flow.
11. Classify each losing-position add as `planned_tranche`, `new_evidence`, `valuation_change`, `price_only`, or `skip`. A `new_evidence` choice must include an evidence delta so the next review can examine it as a thesis event.
12. Focus the narrative on movement against the previous rule and the largest new behavioral leak. Cover every `card_plan.required_honesty_keys` entry with one sentence in `narrative.honesty`. Frame coverage gaps the engine never asked about as neutral facts per `authoring_contract.narrative.unprompted_gaps`, never as the central judgment of the headline or mirror. Do not produce a complete dashboard.
13. After preview, show the card inline following `references/card-delivery.md`, record the actual presentation following `references/interaction-delivery.md`, and only then let the user choose one rule, presenting each candidate's engine-authored `grounding` sentence verbatim with its rule when the payload carries one. Recording `rule_choice_presented` requires the grounding-check evidence described in `references/interaction-delivery.md` (#293); `verify` fails closed if it is missing or shows a non-verbatim match. If the user states their own single-position cap (for example "my limit is 25%"), record it with `review.py set-cap` per `references/data-contract.md` so future reviews reconcile against their number; do not add a card note explaining the threshold. Finalize atomically; update legacy state only through projections.

Do not ask for an already confirmed motive every week. Prepare should requeue it only for a new cycle, new behavior, or an inferred answer that remains the largest contradiction.
