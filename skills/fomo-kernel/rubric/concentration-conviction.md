# Lens: concentration and conviction — v1 draft

This lens distills public Druckenmiller and Soros material. It directly conflicts with risk-capped sizing: high conviction should produce a meaningful bet, while weak conviction should produce no position.

## Five principles

1. Hold cash rather than allocate to low-conviction ideas.
2. Concentrate in the small number of ideas with the strongest evidence.
3. Pay more attention to payoff asymmetry than win rate.
4. Large positions require close attention and fast exits when the thesis changes.
5. Start with macro and liquidity, then choose the expression.
